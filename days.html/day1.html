What is Cyber Security

By: Daisy Chen and Rory Kirtz

People constantly talk about breaches, hacking and people stealing your information through scams and CyberAttacks but very few people know how to protect themselves online. Cybersecurity is the act of protecting devices, software and information online from outside sources. You can protect your information by using tools that can help limit the threats you face online. You may ask yourself, well what about information security? Information security is much broader than cyber security. It considers encryption, endpoint security and physical security and ensures overall protection of data across all environments.

Cyber security is growing more and more important. Being Hacked can be a threat to confidential information, whether that is your own information or the information of large companies and government organizations. Breaches can lead to blackmail, ransom and loss of trust between clients and customers, it can even put companies in legal distress because of stolen information. It is predicted that worldwide by the year of 2028, cyber security will hit $314 billion of spending yearly. Just in one case alone in 2017, 127 million people's credit information was stolen, leading to more and more spending on cybersecurity.

A Cyberattack is a spiteful and illegal effort to access computer systems without others' permission. The purpose of cyber attacks is for people to steal, expose, modify, disable or eradicate information. There are a lot of reasons why people perform cyberattacks, but the most common reason is due to financial means. Attackers often use stolen information to blackmail victims for large sums of money after the attack.

There are 3 main types of cyber attacks.



1 Phishing. Phishing is when victims are lured into something, usually through links. This can happen through emails, websites, social media, texts and much more. The goal of this attack is for the victim to download viruses and malware.

2 Ransomware. Ransomware involves the encryption of data through malware which restricts access to the victims' own files. In order for victims to get their information and files back they are often extorted for money and forced to pay a ransom.

3. Social engineering. Social engineering is when someone tries to uncover sensitive information by tricking or deceiving you. An example would be a person posing as an IT person and calling you in order to get information such as passwords.

There are a lot of sources on who can be behind the cyber attacks. It may come from criminal organizations, state actors and private persons. There are two easy ways to classify these attacks, the outsider versus the insider threats. The outsider versus include organized criminals, professional hackers and amateur hackers. The insider threats include the people that have access to the company’s assets and abuse them deliberately or accidentally.
