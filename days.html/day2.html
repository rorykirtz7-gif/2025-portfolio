Bitcoin

By: Sophia Somogyi and Rory Kirtz

Bitcoin is a term commonly thrown around but what really is it? Bitcoin is a form of Digital Currency with the goal of eliminating the need of central financial authorities like banks or the government. It was the first ever digital currency, made in 2009. There are many different ideas of bitcoin, some believe that it's a scam, some believe that it is the future and others have called it a bubble waiting to pop. Bitcoin despite its many crashes has continued to survive.

Bitcoin has value at the moment, but it is possible for the currency to become completely irrelevant if certain scenarios come to pass. The first is if the Government decided to put restrictions on Bitcoin or ban it altogether. This has already happened in China, which caused the price of Bitcoin to drop by thirty percent in 2021. Therefore, if larger global powers like the United States or European Union did the same, it would greatly decrease the worldwide demand and the overall value of Bitcoin. Additionally, Bitcoin is costly. It uses a very large amount of electricity to mine, around 851.77 kWh of energy per transaction. Due to the growing environmental concerns, it is possible that the government could restrict the mining of Bitcoin in an effort to conserve energy. This decrease in power would leave it vulnerable to attacks and investors would be less likely to buy Bitcoin. Furthermore, if a scandal or the media coverage of Bitcoin leads people to stop believing in Bitcoin, the value would drop drastically. Bitcoin relies on investor confidence, as it does not generate money by itself nor have any sort of intrinsic value. The value of Bitcoin relies on many factors, it is possible for its value to disappear completely.

Bitcoin still has a large monetary value at about $1.6 trillion as of March 2025. There are 4 main reasons as Bitcoin still has value.
Decentralization and security - bitcoin is run through a decentralized network, each transaction is transparent and secured through verified global consensus. Having a decentralized network stops the need for intermediaries which helps lower the chance of fraud and manipulation. Because it is not run through things like banks your transactions can not be controlled or manipulated.
Scarcity- Bitcoin is capped at 21 million coins, meaning that there is a limited supply. Bitcoin mining events have been cut in half which helps slow the supply growth of bitcoin over time.
Institutional Adoption - a lot of companies and governments are buying Bitcoin. The adoption by credible companies give Bitcoin credibility
Use as a store value - in countries with unstable currency many people use bitcoin to help preserve money against hyperinflation.

While Bitcoin losing all value is a possibility, there is no way to know know will happen. The past is an indication that crashes can happen and in that case, money that was worth some amount the previous day can be greatly reduced in a matter of hours. It is a currency that is based on market sentiment, so technically it is possible for it to entirely lose value.
