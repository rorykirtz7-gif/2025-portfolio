AI in the Medical Field

By: Sophia Somogyi and Rory Kirtz

AI has caused technological breakthroughs in many industries. Healthcare has been significantly impacted by the increase in AI use, and it has been causing some concern. The majority of Americans expressed “significant discomfort” about the idea of AI helping to manage their care. However, the impact of AI is only growing, and it is likely going to continue to do so no matter how the general population feels about it. AI has uses that can improve medical care, but there are also risks involved.


There are different types of AI that can be used in healthcare. Predictive AI predicts the future with mathematical models and pattern recognition. It could be used to review the profiles of patients with pneumonia and predict which are most likely to require hospitalization. The algorithm could use factors like age, gender, medical history, and background to make suggestions to doctors about the best treatment plan. Generative AI uses large language models to interact with users in a human-like manner. It may be possible for these models to be trained to be able to pass medical licensing exams. A negative is that these LLMs can ‘hallucinate’ and produce incorrect responses, depending on the data that the AI was trained on, which is why it is important that the AI be used as a tool to help doctors, but not be given the ability to make any kind of final decisions.


One way AI is already being used for diagnosing patients is with colorectal cancer. A colonoscopy is a procedure done by physicians to look and remove potentially cancerous polyps. AI can be trained to recognize these polyps and identify them. It has been found that using AI for this has reduced the rate of missing these cancerous areas. Another example is with reading mammograms, a screening tool that is used to detect breast cancer. AI has been trained and found to be at least as accurate as two trained radiologists. However, the cost of doing so has caused limitations on where it can be used.


One case of ai being used for treatment is ai that was developed at Johns Hopkins University, it was used to identify patients who are at high risk of developing Sepsis ( a life-threatening condition when the body’s immune system overreacts to infection, potentially causing organ damage, shock or death). AI has been used to limit the time it takes to identify sepsis in a patient so doctors can start antibiotics and other life-saving treatments earlier. AI has also been used to look for signs of deterioration in a patient's condition. IT looks at things such as vital signs, lab tests and reports to look for wording in overall condition. If this is detected Then it will alert a staff member to do an in-person evaluation of the patient.


A lot of people are worried about their privacy surrounding AI in hospitals. AI has been used for long periods of time within the medical field, so you should always feel free to ask your service provider about any concerns. Many times you sign a waiver before entering a hospital or other treatment facilities that will ask for permission about certain technology. Despite this all your medical information is protected by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act also known as HIPAA.

AI can be extremely beneficial because it helps reduce the time spent doing things such as paperwork. AI is sometimes used to listen to the conversations between a doctor and their patient to write notes about the interaction that doctors can later edit. They have also been using AI to write letters to insurance companies about treatments instead of doing it themselves. This help from AI generally helps healthcare workers with burnout.
