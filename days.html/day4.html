IoT Killer Apps

By: Sophia somogyi and Rory Kirtz

The Internet of Things is no longer the novelty that it used to be. People have become accustomed to having smart devices and technology in their lives. Tech trends like platform based devices, AI’s use of real world data, and product development have made IoT become more profit focused and have caused a wave of killer apps.

A killer app is an application that is so valuable that it makes underlying products and technologies indispensable. IoT helps create killer apps. A killer IoT application uses connected devices to create industry - changing benefits. None of the apps IoT has helped create have gained killer status yet.

Artificial Intelligence has caused a lot of new opportunities to be created. IoT devices provide real world operations data that is used to train AI. This data can then be used to automate operations. Killer IoT apps can be created in industries that need an interaction between the real world and AI applications. This can include smart homes, manufacturing, agriculture, healthcare, and more.

There have been many barriers regarding IoT and killer apps. Extracting business value from large streams of diverse data is difficult and costs a large amount of money. There is not a huge demand for the product Iot companies do not directly work with customers, they focus too little on the bigger picture of their products.

Three new trends have come up that are now combating these barriers :

1. There is a growing demand for operations data, ai helps simplify the processing of machine found data.
2.IoT devices are evolving to platform based approaches .
3.IoT companies are changing focus from connecting everything to creating killer apps.

1. The first AI trend that improves IoT is how it is becoming increasingly more used, which means more operational data is gathered, especially in industrial processes and physical actions. Automation and optimization of killer apps will create an unending demand for data. Additionally, AI models can be trained with diverse data types, sources and formats can reduce the need for the data to be interpreted and sorted before being processed. AI is also being implemented directly into small IoT devices, reducing cloud computing costs, cutting response time, and addressing problems more efficiently.

2. The second AI trend that improves IoT would be platform-Based,Software Defined IoT Devices.There us a strong demand for process automation which creates opportunities for IoT based products. Adding embedded hardware and software can cause a lot of issues because it requires customization which can cause technical debt for companies. Despite this, IoT devices still require customization. Moder IoT devices require high-level coding languages and other advanced features and leaving these customizations for individual devices is unsustainable.

3. The third trend in AI is product-centric IoT devices. There are many connected industrial products and eventually, IoT technology will eventually be advanced and accurate enough that a few suppliers will be able to account for all project needs rather than having to use many different and less professional devices. It is a shift from IoT being technology-centric to being product-centric. However, this shift is difficult for companies to make because IoT itself is not a final product, but an ingredient. Product-centric IoT suppliers will embrace device platforms which are AI-enabled and software-defined.
